Our Purpose

Our main purpose and goal for this AI is to help users take the best steps possible to treat any health issues the plant may be battling. At some point, every beginner and experienced plant parent runs into an issue that they are unable to diagnose, let alone treat. Our AI will identify, diagnose, and provide solutions to various fruit plant concerns. Keeping your fruit plants alive and well has just become much easier.

Work Process

Our Computer Vision AI journey throughout these 3 weeks and the process!

Step 1: Scraping Data

The first step in the process of data accumulation was using SerpAPI and the Beautiful Soup HTML parser. These services were used to scrape data from the Google Search Engine and specific websites. Additionally, we used a dataset available on Kaggle, resulting in a total of about 7,500+ in entirety.

Step 2: Cleaning and Labeling the Data

It was then necessary to label the data through the RoboFlow application and clean accordingly. Since we had a two-step procedure for our product, we ended up at a total of 13 projects/models. This included the main identification model as well as the various species of fruits. Altogether, there are a total of about 13,500 images cumulatively including the augmentation steps.

Step 3: Training the Models

Using the YOLOv5 algorithm and Google Colab, our models were trained. Throughout the training process, input parameters such as batch size or the number of epochs altered. Eventually, we implemented the identification model and all diagnosis models where we then received metric data and respective PyTorch files.

Step 4: Interface and Website Integration

We then developed a website through CoCalc consisting of the necessary HTML, CSS(Some Bootstrap), and Javascript code for the frontend. As the backend, we integrated the Python Flask framework and included our PyTorch model files. Unfortunately, only 10 out of the 14 diagnosis models were finally used due to differences in accuracy. Overall, our model takes an image of a plant, provides an output, takes an image of a diseased leaf of the plant, and provides a diagnosis and solution.

Challenges

    As expected, we ran into a few challenges:

  • The large amount of data (13,000+ images) that our two models require took a lot of time to label.
  • We had around 14 Roboflow Projects that all needed to be trained.
  • Lack of quality data for diagnosis.
  • Diagnosis accuracy is much lower than Identification accuracy.

Improvements

    Some improvements we could've made are...

  • Including sufficient amounts of high-quality data for each fruit's disease.
  • Focusing more time on the diagnosis as much as we did for the Idenfication accuracy.
  • Optimizing more time by searching for quality pre-labeled datasets.


    • Additions we could add to our project:

  • Increasing the amount of plant species that we can identify.
  • Adding the majority of the diseases for each plant to diagnose.
  • Adding more unique datasets to increase accuracy.
  • Adding more solution resources for the user.
